Home Inspection 101

What a home inspection is—and how to choose the right inspector.

A home inspection is a professional visual assessment of a property's major systems and safety concerns. It helps buyers understand the home’s current condition, likely maintenance needs, and possible repair risks before closing.

What gets inspected

  • Roof, attic, structure, and foundation clues
  • Electrical panels, outlets, and visible wiring
  • Plumbing fixtures, drainage, and water heater setup
  • HVAC operation and visible duct condition
  • Doors, windows, insulation, and built-in appliances

What buyers should ask an inspector

  • Are you licensed and insured in this state?
  • Do you have certification and continuing education?
  • Can I attend the inspection and ask questions?
  • How fast do I receive the report and does it include photos?
  • Do you explain severity: urgent, soon, and routine?

Checklist before you book

  • Sample report that is clear and easy to scan
  • Transparent pricing and what add-ons include
  • Scope details and exclusions in writing
  • Strong local reviews and agent/buyer referrals
  • A communication style that helps you feel confident
